# Nan Coffee, Please — Pitching Deck Brief and Image-Prompt Guide

## Deck objective

Present **Nan Coffee, Please** as an AI coffee-discovery product that makes Nan's lesser-known specialty cafés, Nan-grown beans, and work-friendly café spaces easier to discover in every season.

The central message:

> One great coffee can lead to a fuller, more local journey through Nan.

## Audience and track

- Audience: Nan Beyond Seasons Challenge Hackathon judges
- Primary track: **Track 1 — AI Trip Planner & Concierge**
- Supporting proof points: workation-friendly café discovery, local merchant empowerment, responsible AI, and all-season local value distribution

## Visual identity

- Tone: warm, premium, contemporary, quiet, and locally grounded
- Palette: espresso brown, cream, forest green, muted indigo, and a restrained saffron accent
- Visual materials: coffee paper, mountain mist, hand-drawn route lines, subtle woven-textile geometry inspired by Nan
- Use verified photos of real cafés/beans/places whenever possible
- Use generated images only as atmosphere or abstract illustration, never as a factual depiction of a real café, menu, local event, or person
- Include generous negative space; keep text added in Canva/Slides, not inside generated images

## Global image-prompt suffix

> editorial travel photography or refined digital illustration, authentic northern Thailand atmosphere, warm natural light, contemporary specialty-coffee mood, premium muted earth-tone palette, generous negative space for presentation text, no readable text, no logos, no watermarks

## Narrative arc

```text
Hidden cafés and changing coffee stories
        ↓
Travelers only find what is already popular
        ↓
AI helps them start from a real plan, taste, or current location
        ↓
Badges make Nan coffee and work-friendly spaces visible
        ↓
Voice-first merchant updates keep the local data trustworthy
        ↓
More local discovery in every season
```

## Copy guardrails

- Do not claim coffee alone creates low-season travel demand; position it as the first stop that unlocks deeper local discovery.
- Do not call merchant-submitted speed results a guarantee. Show a timestamp and trust label.
- Distinguish Nan-grown from Nan-roasted coffee.
- Use only verified local facts, partner information, and moderated reviews.
- State that AI drafts, translates, matches, and explains; merchants approve and humans moderate.

## 90-second demo story

1. A traveler selects “I already have a plan” and says that they will be at Wat Phumin in the afternoon.
2. The app recommends a short-detour café because it has a Nan-grown Natural light roast and is open now.
3. The traveler sees the café's Workation Friendly and Speed reported details, current menu pick, and why it matches.
4. The app offers one verified lesser-known nearby place that fits the remaining time and today's conditions.
5. At the café, the traveler checks in and later leaves a structured review.
6. The café owner speaks a new-bean update in Thai; AI structures it, drafts bilingual content, and waits for owner approval.
7. Once approved, the public profile and future trail recommendations become fresher.
